\begin{abstract}
  In this paper we give a positive answer to an open problem recently posed by Kavvos: is there any categorical interpretation of Gödel-Löb axiom~$\Box (\Box A \to A) \to \Box A$ but refuting~$A \to \Box A$?
  We consider the ordinary category $\Asm(\Lambda)$ of assemblies on untyped $\lambda$-calculus and \emph{maps with trackers} similar to the $\PER$-category $\ASM(\mathcal{A})$ of assemblies on an arbitrary partial combinatory algebra $\mathcal{A}$ proposed previously. 
  Then, we construct an endofunctor as the interpretation of the (provability) modality~$\Box$.
  Of the utmost significance of our understanding for $\Box A$ is that its denotation should involve not only extensions but also intensions, e.g., derivations of a proof, code of programs, or $\lambda$-terms \emph{modulo $\alpha$-equivalence only}, inspired by its provability reading.
  Our results are established in a cubical type theory extended with guarded recursion and formalised in Guarded Cubical Agda.
  Apart from its own theoretical interest, following the interpretation of $\Box A$ as a type of typed code \`a la Davies and Pfenning, our work is a step towards extending type theories on which proof assistants are based with non-structural but guarded recursion on the meta level in a logically consistent way.
\end{abstract}
